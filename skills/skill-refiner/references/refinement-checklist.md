# Refinement Checklist

- preserve the original skill intent
- prefer evidence from evals, feedback, and traces over intuition
- tighten the trigger language
- remove ambiguity from instructions
- keep the output contract stable when possible
- explain why a new instruction exists if the reason is not obvious
- keep the skill portable and minimal
- remove weak or redundant instructions if they do not improve results
- call out repeated work that should move into scripts, references, or examples
- avoid broadening scope during refinement
